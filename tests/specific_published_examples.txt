# Externally Validated Payroll Examples for Kenya 2026

## Sources
- Net Pay Kenya (https://netpay.co.ke)
- HR Fleek (https://hrfleek.co.ke)

## Validation Status
These examples have been verified against third-party payroll calculators
and represent authoritative reference calculations for our system.

---

## NSSF Year 4 Parameters (Effective February 2026)

- Lower Earnings Limit (LEL/Tier I): KES 9,000
- Upper Earnings Limit (UEL/Tier II): KES 108,000 (approx. 3x National Average)
- Contribution Rate: 6% employee + 6% employer
- Maximum Employee Contribution: KES 6,480 (540 + 5,940)

## Example 1: Entry Level (KES 30,000)

### Input
- Gross Monthly Salary: KES 30,000

### Output (Validated)
| Item           | Amount (KES) | Notes                    |
|----------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| NSSF           | 1,800.00     | 6% of Gross (Below UEL)  |
| SHIF           | 825.00       | 2.75% of Gross           |
| Housing Levy   | 450.00       | 1.5% of Gross            |
| Taxable Pay    | 26,925.00    | Gross - (NSSF+SHIF+AHL)  |
| PAYE Tax       | 732.00       | On Taxable, less relief  |
| **Net Pay**    | **26,193.00**|                          |

## Key Regulatory Notes

1. **NSSF Deductibility**: Employee NSSF contribution is an allowable deduction
   (reduces taxable income)

2. **SHIF Deductibility**: SHIF is an allowable deduction (reduces taxable income)

3. **AHL Deductibility**: Affordable Housing Levy is an allowable deduction
   (reduces taxable income)

4. **Personal Relief**: KES 2,400/month is subtracted from gross PAYE

5. **Year 4 Transition**: These rates apply from February 2026 onwards.
   January 2026 uses Year 3 rates (LEL=8,000, UEL=72,000).
